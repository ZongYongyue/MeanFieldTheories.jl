# Single-Mode Approximation

This page introduces a theory of collective excitations on top of a
Hartree-Fock ground state, which is commonly called the **single-mode
approximation** (SMA) or **Bethe-Salpeter
equation** (BSE).

---

## Collective Excitations in Many-Body Systems: A Background Introduction

In condensed matter physics, understanding the excited states of a many-body system is as crucial as determining its ground state. While **Hartree-Fock (HF)** theory provides a reliable description of the ground state $|G\rangle$ and single-particle quasiparticle spectra, it fails to capture **collective modes**—correlated states involving the coherent motion of many particles. 

To describe these excitations, we typically construct a "particle-hole" (p-h) subspace, where an electron is promoted from an occupied band (valence) to an unoccupied band (conduction).

### 1. The Single Mode Approximation (SMA)

The **Single Mode Approximation** is a variational approach used to estimate the energy of collective modes. It assumes that at a given momentum $\mathbf{q}$, the entire spectral weight is concentrated into a single excitation mode.

#### Core Concept
We assume the excited state $|\Psi_{\mathbf{q}}\rangle$ is generated by a specific collective operator $O_{\mathbf{q}}$ acting on the ground state:
$$|\Psi_{\mathbf{q}}\rangle = O_{\mathbf{q}} |G\rangle$$
In magnetic systems, $O_{\mathbf{q}}$ is typically a spin-flip operator $S^\dagger_{\mathbf{q}}$; in quantum liquids, it is the density fluctuation operator $\rho_{\mathbf{q}}$.

#### The Bijl-Feynman Formula
Using the variational principle, the excitation energy $\Delta(\mathbf{q})$ is given by the ratio of the frequency-weighted sum rule to the static structure factor:
$$\Delta(\mathbf{q}) = \frac{\langle G| [O_{\mathbf{q}}^\dagger, [H, O_{\mathbf{q}}]] |G\rangle}{2\langle G| O_{\mathbf{q}}^\dagger O_{\mathbf{q}} |G\rangle}$$
While SMA is computationally efficient and provides an upper bound for the excitation energy, it lacks the flexibility to describe systems where multiple bands or complex orbital structures contribute differently to the excitation.

### 2. The Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE)

The **Bethe-Salpeter Equation** can be viewed as a generalized SMA. Instead of "guessing" the form of the excitation operator, the BSE allows the system to determine the optimal superposition of all possible particle-hole pairs via a linear eigenvalue problem.

#### The Physical Imagery: Ladder Approximation
The BSE describes the repeated scattering between a created electron and the hole it left behind. In the language of Feynman diagrams, this corresponds to a summation of **ladder diagrams**. The electron and hole are "bound" together by an effective interaction, forming a collective boson (e.g., an exciton or a magnon).



#### Mathematical Structure
Starting from the HF ground state, the collective state $|\chi, \mathbf{q}\rangle$ is expanded as:
$$|\chi, \mathbf{q}\rangle = \sum_{\mathbf{k}, v, c} z_{\mathbf{k}, v, c} f^\dagger_{\mathbf{k+q}, c} f_{\mathbf{k}, v} |G\rangle$$
Minimizing the energy $\langle \chi, \mathbf{q} | \hat{H} | \chi, \mathbf{q} \rangle$ leads to the BSE eigenvalue equation:
$$(E_{\mathbf{k+q}, c} - E_{\mathbf{k}, v}) z_{\mathbf{k}} + \sum_{\mathbf{p}} K(\mathbf{k, p, q}) z_{\mathbf{p}} = \Omega_{\mathbf{q}} z_{\mathbf{k}}$$
* **Quasiparticle Term:** $(E_c - E_v)$ represents the energy cost to create a free electron-hole pair.
* **Interaction Kernel:** $K(\mathbf{k, p, q})$ (the "Kernel") accounts for the attractive interaction between the electron and the hole, which lowers the excitation energy below the single-particle continuum.

### 3. Historical Origins & Landmark Literature

The development of these methods represents a cornerstone of many-body theory:

#### Foundations of Quantum Liquids
* **Feynman, R. P. (1954).** *Atomic Theory of the Two-Fluid Model of Liquid Helium.* [Phys. Rev. 94, 262].
    * *Significance:* Introduced the SMA to explain the phonon-roton spectrum in superfluid Helium-4.

#### The Bound State Problem in QED
* **Salpeter, E. E., & Bethe, H. A. (1951).** *A Relativistic Equation for Bound-State Problems.* [Phys. Rev. 84, 1232].
    * *Significance:* The original derivation of the BSE for the two-body bound state problem.

#### Collective Excitations in Topological Phases
* **Girvin, S. M., MacDonald, A. H., & Platzman, P. M. (1986).** *Magneto-roton theory of collective excitations in the fractional quantum Hall effect.* [Phys. Rev. B 33, 2481].
    * *Significance:* Developed the **GMP theory**, applying SMA to the Fractional Quantum Hall Effect (FQHE).

#### Modern Condensed Matter Applications
* **Onida, G., Reining, L., & Rubio, A. (2002).** *Electronic excitations: density-functional versus many-body Green’s-function approaches.* [Rev. Mod. Phys. 74, 601].
    * *Significance:* The definitive review on implementing the $GW+BSE$ framework for real materials.
